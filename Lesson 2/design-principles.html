Visual Hierarchy

Airbnb

https://www.airbnb.com/

Airbnb is a good example of use of Visual Hierarchy because its layout allows the user to to easily see how to operate the site. The meny options are laid out in a simple way. Below the menu options you see the location, check-in, check-out dates, number of adults and children options as well as the amenities that you prefer and then the search button. These are all laid out nicely and everything below that is an option to become an Airbnb host and then customer reviews including images and vacation places that were just recently booked. The order of importance is well used in the website. Nothing seems out of order or hard to understand or find. I feel that Airbnb has delivers a good user experience because of how it is set up.

White Space and Clean Design

Hobby Lobby

https://www.hobbylobby.com/

Although it is not the most appealing, I feel that Hobby Lobby has done a decent job on leaving enough white space but not too much so that their site isn't overwhelming because it is jammed full of images, adds, menu options, etc.

Rule of Thirds

Mule Deer Foundation

https://muledeer.org/

I feel that the mule deer foundation website implements the rule of thirds well because they have their images and content laid out in a way that indicates that they used the rule of thirds grid pattern and this makes the website more appealing to look at and scroll through. They do not have images all over the page on all sides of the site and in the middle. It is laid out in a well defined rule of thirds manner.
